QUALITY POLICY

We, at Plato’s Academy, strive to bring out and nurture the talents and skills of youth with quality education, motivate them to be self-disciplined and develop their competence to face the challenges of the world . A learning journey is a strategic approach to developing group of leaders over time. It is based on the principle that true behaviour changes take time and that people learn the best together.

Mission Statement

PLATO‟S ACADEMY endeavours to promote all-round development of the child through teaming for life. We achieve this by providing internationally recognized standards of education with an Indian ethos in a culturally rich atmosphere. Our aim is to give individual attention to every child so that he / she has maximum opportunity to excel.

Objectives

Instil a strong academic base. Provide an education that prepares the student for life, ensuring his/her holistic development. Provide students with a positive, stimulating, and caring environment that encourages the fulfilment of individual potential and the growth of self-esteem in academic, social, and personal areas and overcome challenges in the future world.

Our Legacy

Founded in 1985 by Mrs. Kareena Lopez and Mr. Conrad Lopez, Plato's Academy began in Tiruppur with a powerful mission: to provide every child with academic excellence and fluent, confident command of the English language. At that time, Tiruppur was a small town with limited educational opportunities, making Plato’s Academy a beacon for quality learning. Starting from humble beginnings, the academy quickly earned a name for itself, renowned for its strong foundation in language education within just five years of establishment.

  • Providing excellent infrastructure and conducive learning environment

  • Responding to the changes both in technology and applications

  • Associating with leading academic institutions

Academic Policies

PRE-PRIMARY [LKG AND UKG]

At this level, the student’s skills are assessed based on the progress of the child. There will be periodic meetings with individual parents as and when required. The parent will have to consult the teachers and Head of School in the event of the student’s non-performance, and the best possible solution will be discussed. The school will make the final decision in the student’s promotion.

Learn More

GRADE 1 TO GRADE 4

At this level, there are Continuous and Formative Assessments. The teacher maintains detailed reports on each student’s progress. The parent will consult the teachers and Head of School in the event of the student not being able to reach the expected level of the course. Keeping the student in mind, the best possible solution will be discussed. The school will make the final decision in the student’s promotion.

Learn More

GRADE 5 TO GRADE 8

At this level, there are Continuous and Formative Assessments which comprise the internal grade. At the end of the semester, there is a Summative Assessment which tests the entire semester’s learning. These marks form the exam grade. On the Report Card, the final grade is a calculation of the Continuous Formative Assessment and the exam grades, weighted at 60% and 40% respectively.

Learn More

GRADE 9 TO GRADE 12

At this level, there are Continuous and Formative Assessments which together form the Internal Grade. At the end of the Semester, there is a Summative Assessment that tests the entire term/year’s learning and these marks form the Exam Grade. On the Report Card, the student will receive two grades, one on internal performance and the other one on their performance in the final exam.

Learn More

PREDICTED SCORES FOR GRADE 10 TO 12

The predicted scores will be based on the grades achieved in their internal assessment of the current academic year. Students will not be allowed to appear for the term-end exams if: They do not have the required percentage of attendance, Fee has not been cleared.

Learn More

WITHDRAWAL AND TC POLICY

In case of withdrawal of a child from the institution, the parents well in advance with the written format should notify the authorities A 2-month notice period is mandatory. The parents should ensure that all the dues have been cleared.

Why Choose Us

REGULARITY

All students are required to be regular to school. They must have a minimum attendance. Students who are lagging behind in their attendance quotient will be warned and parents will be given written information of the same. Students who do not achieve this minimum will not be allowed to attempt term exams.

Punctuality

School begins at 8:45 am sharp. Students are expected to be in school before the bell rings Students will not be allowed into a class when late unless they have authorized written permission for their delay

Home Work Assignment Policy

Homework is given to students to reinforce the learning that has taken place in the classroom and to help the student practice skills which are specific to the subject. By doing so, the student is more confident and comfortable with the subject. Homework should be done on a regular basis

Aggression

Students will be courteous to others and will respect school and personal property. Hazing, Bullying, Intimidation, etc. will not be tolerated. Offences of this nature are considered serious and will be dealt accordingly.
